Jahr. Allgem .Deutsch. Orn. Gesell.

	Jahresvers.Allgem.Deutsch.Orn.Gesell.
Note: This citation is a problem, and may be an index to a much larger problem. I currently (2021.01.29) have two citations to this source:

  • Tarphonomus harterti (Berlepsch) 1892 Jahr.Allgem.Deutsch.Orn.Gesell. p.10
  • Crypturellus atrocapillus garleppi (Berlepsch) 1892 Jahr.Allgem.Deutsch.Orn.Gesell. p.13

    • I can infer the basis for using one, but must speculate (so far) for the other. Conventionally these are give as:

  • Tarphonomus harterti (Berlepsch) 1892 J.Orn. 40 no.200 p.452
  • Crypturellus atrocapillus garleppi (Berlepsch) 1892 J.Orn. 40 no.200 p.454

    • These pages in the J.Orn. are (as I understand it) copies of the Jahr.Allgem.Deusch.Gesell. so the questions are:

    • was this material published PRIOR to its being printed in J.Orn.
      • if so,
    • what do we know about the date of that publication?
    • Was this occurance unique to this year, or other other years where taxa listed in J.Orn. within this portion of the volume need to be cited to the report of the meeting.?

    Evidence for publication of Jarh.Allgem.Deutsch.Orn.Gesell. separate from the J.Orn.

    • The Cat.BirdsBr.Mus. 27:533 Has this:

      "15. Crypturus garleppi.
      Crypturus garleppi, Berlepsch, Ber. Allg. Deutschl. Orn. Ges. Dec.
      1892, p. 13 (Santa Cruz de la Sierra, Bolivia); id. J. f. 0. 1892, p. 454."

    • This would seem (unequivocally) to indicate a separate, and presumably initial, publication prior to the J.Orn. publication.
    • Additionally, it is my interpretation that the relevant number (no.200) of the J.Orn. was not published until 1893. (v.i. Current evidence indicates no.200 published Apr. 3, 1893).
    • The problem, then, is of unknown scope. How frequently were new names published in separately and initially released Yearly reports?

		to recognize.

		The timing of this distribution (which would certainly appear to satisfy the requirements
		for being a "publication") is at present (2020.04.06) unkown.

	Thus H&M follows Peters' inconsistencies in the dates of taxa from this volume (7)
	and number (42) dating it sometimes to 1859 (which we can assume is the imprint date) and
	once (Illadopsis) to 1860.

	The evidence in support of 1860 for all taxa from "no.42" includes:
		1.) CWR's date and his note suggesting there is evidence on p.463
		2.) Deignan's use of 1860 for Illadopsis (but he very well may simply 
			have been following Richmond).
		3.) Neave's apparently independant use of 1860.
		4.) Shulze et al. using not just 1860, but dating this to July of 1860.
		5.) Number 40 of this volume was accessioned by the Smithsonian on
			May 19, 1860 and thus it seems quite unlikely that even the numerically
			preceeding number was published in 1859 (though it must be noted that
			not only were the J.Orn. numbers usually long-delayed from their
			"specified dates" but also the sequence of publication does not always 
			match the numerical order).

	The 1860 VII indicates a publishing date of July, 1860 for the November 1859 issue
	of J.Orn. (a delay of 8-9 months), and thus we can understand the consistent, contemporaneous,
	complaints about delayed publication of the Journal.
