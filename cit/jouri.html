IndischeZool.

	Indische Zoologie ... Nebst einer kurzen vorlaeufigen Abhandlung
	ueber den Umfang von Indien und die Beschaffenheit des Klima, des
	Bodens und des Meeres daselbst (-Von den Paradiesvoegeln und dem
	Phoenix) und einem Anhange (Specimen Faunulae Indicae auctore T
	Pennant), darin ein kurzes Verzeichniss der Thiere in Indien
	mitgetheilt wird = Zoologia Indica, &c

	Forster, Johann Reinhold [1729-1798]
	1781	
	Halle.
	fol.	
	[viii,] iv, 42p : 15 pls ; fol.

	[NOTE: Based on the first edition of Pennant's "Indian
     	Zoology".  Twelve plates published in Pennant's work, and three
     	unpublished ones, were given to Forster when he left England. 
	Foster gave Pennant's text a German translation, prefixed the
     	description of India, and added the section on the Bird of
     	Paradise and the Phoenix. These new portions were then translated 
	into English for the second edition of Pennant's work.
	Forster's text is German and Latin in parallel columns.]

ref. Kertesz GA. "Notes on Isis Von Oken, 1817-1848" 1986. Isis 77:497-503

	Apparently the Journal was initially published weekly, but later 12 issues per year were published.
	"The first issue was published in August 1816, but dated 1817." (p.499)
	"... in weekly parts 9 though 11, in October 1816, he [Oken] published his first political article." (p.499)
	One issue, that reported on the Wartburg Festival in Oct. 1817, was confiscated by the government.
	Following this act, the figures on the masthead were changed with the figure of Osiris being replaced 
	by Isis's son Harpocrates (Horus). The interpretation is that Horus' raised hand is interpreted
	to command silence. Additionally, a figure drawn to illustrate a report of Wied-Neiuwied's expedition
	to Brasil was redrawn and shown with a padlock through his protruding lips. This appeared as a "vignette"
	in several issues later on.

Title:

	The initial contract specified that the title would be 
		"Encyklopädische Blätter"
	It appeared in August 1816 as
		"Isis oder Encyklopädische Zeitung" (Jg. 1817 - Jg. 1827)

	Kertesz maintains that "It was only in 1833 that Isis was given,
	on the title page of the monthly issues (though not on the the annual volume),
	a new subtitle: "Encyclopedic Journal Especially for Natural History,
	Comparative Anatomy, and Physiology."

	Hölzinger 1991 "Die Vö Baden-Wüttemburgs Band 7 Bibliographie" p.153 
	indicates, in contrast, that the title became
	"Isis Enzyklopädische Zeitwchrift, vorzüglich fü Naturgeschichte,
	 vergleichende Anatomie und Physiologie." 
	in 1827

	Apparently there were both monthly and annual title pages.
