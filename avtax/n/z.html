Zoological Citation Notes --Z

Zeitsch.ges.Orn Heft 4 Date
  • This Heft is conventionally dated to 1885 (the imprint date).
  • Two pieces of evidence support the interpretation of a delay in publication.
    1. The receipt date at the MCZ Mayr librar is written as "March 9, 1886" (see BHDL copy).
    2. On p.369 Finsch refers to the 1886 volume of The Ibis, citing Donacicola hunsteini (now) Lonchura hunstseini which was not published until 1886.
  • A very real possibility is that Finsch KNEW in 1885 of the details of the Ibis publication and included the reference on that basis. However, this consititutes imaginative speculation, and unless supported by facts is not appropriate (in my view) for the dating of scientific names.

Campylorhynchus zonatus Type Locality
  • Lesson gives the type locality as "Californie".
  • As Peters Checklist 9385 (= Paynter 1960) notes, Brodkorb subsitutes Orizaba, Mexico for the type locality.
  • Paynter, however cites Brodkorb to "1942, Condor, 49, p. 242." a citation that clearly can not be correct. Examination of the Condor, shows that the typographic error is in the date, which should be 1947, not 1942.

Phasianus colchicus zerafschanicus Citation
  • Peters CL 2:123 dates this to 1893 without comment.
  • This is followed, also without discussion, by H&M 3rd:60 and HBW 2:543.
  • The Richmond Index, shows the date for this publication as 1891, which I follow.
  • Thanks to Mark Brown for bringing this to my attention.
  • Mark Brown also provides further support for the 1891 date in that on p.219 of "Sporting Guns and Gunpowders... published in the "Field" newspaper,..." notes that Vol.78 of "Field" was published on Sept. 12, 1891, making 1893 an extremely unlikely date for volume 77.
  • In addition Seebohm in the Ibis on p.270 of the April 5 1892 issue discusses this bird indicating it was published on the 21st of March 1891 in the Newpaper "Field". The date of 1893, as used by Peters, H&M 3rd:60, and HBW 2:543 appears insupportable.
  • Edward Dickinson confirms (in litt. 2009.07.25) that this is in vol.77, no.1995 and that it was published in 1891.

Hedydipna collaris zambesiana Citation
  • There is variability in the way this citation is given:
    1. The Richmond Index has pt. I July 28, 1876 p.345 pl.112
    2. Peters CL 12:220 has p.343 pl.111
    3. CBBM 9:117 has p.343 p. 111 fig.3
    4. Sclater 1930 Syst.Av.Aeth. 2:708 has p.243 pl. iii
    5. The Type list for BrMus. gives p.343 pl.111
  • Dr David Donsker examined his personal copy, noting that pages and the plates (and even the figures) are not numbered. The references, as given come from the front matter, which Dr Donsker confirmed by direct count in his copy.
  • The discrepant count by Richmond raises the possibility that all sets may not be bound in exactly the same way.

Ptilinopus perlatus zonurus Citation
  • Originally described in Ptilopus and for this reason H&M 3rd:175 and HBW 4:209 place the authority in parentheses.
  • It appears to me that Ptilopus is an 1841 Strickland emendation of Swainson's Ptilinopus. As an emendation it appears to me that it implies that the authority here should not be in parentheses.

Tyto alba punctatissima Date
  • This name is frequently (e.g. Peters Checklist 4:82; H&M 3 rd:219; HBW 5:71) listed as 1838.
  • The Richmond Index shows that this is in "no.9" of Pt III, and shows a date of July, 1839.
  • Zimmer 1926 cites Sherborn's work on this {Ann.Mag.Nat.Hist.,(6) 20, p.483} where it is shown that this was published in July of 1839.

Zosterops Case
  • The ICZN initially in 1985 [Art 30(a)(ii)] and reaffirmed in 2000 [Art. 30.1.4.3] treats gender in this manner:
    • "A compound genus-group name ending in -ops is to be treated as masculine, regardless of its derivation or of its treatment by its author."
  • Zosterops is a compound formed from Gr. zoster - belt or girdle, and ops - the eye.
