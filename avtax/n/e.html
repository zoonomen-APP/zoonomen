Edolisoma Nomenclature
  • Generic revisions, motivated and guided predominantly by molecular studies have resulted in this name being returned to use.
  • These recent careful studies appear to have restricted their attention to taxonomic matters.
  • It seems that the nomenclatural research was restricted to looking the name up in its listing in synonomy in Peters IX:168 1960 (apparently Mayr), where the name, in a synonomic list of 22 (!) genus group names, has the authority listed as "Jacquin and Pucheran".
  • Apparently overlooked then, and certainly now, is Zimmer's 1932 treatment (p.186) of this work, where he notes: 
    		"Although Jacquinot's name appears on the subtitle page, a statement in the 
		introduction (obviously by Pucheran although unsigned) announces that neither Hombron
		nor Jacquinot is to be held responsible for errors in the nomeclature 
		adopted in the succeeding text for which he (Pucheran) is alone to blame. It is 
		evident that Pucheran must be quoted as author of the ornithology and mammalogy."

Quelea erythrops Citation

    
	
  • Peters Checklist 15:61 (= Moreau 1962) cites this to "pl.109".
    • 
	
  • The name occurs on page 109, and there is no plate.
    • 
	
  • Sclater WL. 1930 Systema Avium Aethopicarum p.758 cites this name
	to "pl.109", while Sharpe. 1890 CBBM 13:255 correctly cites it to "p.109".
	Similarly Sherborn in Index Animalium cites this name to "109" and would have
	specifically designated it as a plate if he felt it was a plate.
    • 
	
  • It appears likely that Moreau, at least in this instance, looked at only one
	secondary source, and did not evaluate either the original material
	or other easily available citations for this name.
    • 
	
  • This, of course, is common practise, and understandably so. In my own case
	it is infrequent that I am able to check the original material though increased 
	availability of material on the internet is changing this. I do think, however,
	that it is unwise to consistently rely on only one secondary source.

Chalcomitra adelberti eboensis Citation

    

  • Cited by Peters Checklist 12:236 to 1843 and this is followed universally.
    • 

  • Sheets-Pyenson, Susan. 1981. "War and Peace in Natural History Publishing: The Naturalist's
Library, 1833-1843." ISIS 72:50-72. addresses this work in detail. From her study of
Jardine's correspondence she determines that this work was published in Dec. 1842.
    • 

  • I am unaware of any reason not to believe her interpretation, which appears to have been
universally ignored by the ornithological community.
    • 

  • Iredale also published on this work (Iredale. 1951. "'The Naturalist's Library: an Essay in Bibliography.'
Australian Zoologist 11:322-332.) and Edward Dickinson kindly provided me a copy to study. It is clear that
uncertainty remains as to whether this volume was published in Dec. 1842, or in 1843. Possible explanations can be
provided for either case. The appearance (but not the certainty) is that Sheets-Pyenson may have dated this based on
information in the Jardine correspondence (which was material unknown to Iredale), and Sheets-Pyenson was fully aware of
and relied partly Iredale's work when she published her paper. For the present time I am employing the dating she
published on p.72 of her work.
    •

Erpornis Nomenclature

    

  • H&M 3rd p.622 evidently interprets
Herpornis to be a valid genus independant of Erpornis, and not an
Agassiz 1846 emendation of Blyth's 1844 name.
    • 

  • My work suggests that Neave, Schulze (et al.), Richmond, Gray (1869
Hand-List Genera and Species of birds  Part 1 p.315), and Salvin (1882
Cat.Coll.Birds. Hugh Strickland p.73) all see the original Erpornis spelling
as subsequently modified (emended). I follow these authors and interpret
Herpronis to be an emendation of Erpornis, and thus no parenteses for
the authority are required if the original combination was made in Herpornis.
(Code 1999 Art. 51.3.1).
    • 

  • Scudder Nomenclator Zoologicus Part II p.146 lists "Herpornis Hodgs. Aves
1844", but I believe this is an error.

Eupetes Systematics

    

  • Peters Checklist 10:238 (Deignan, 1964) treats Eupetes in the
MUSCICAPIDAE and places it following Ptilorrhoa.
    • 

  • Jonsson et al. 2007. Biol. Lett. find that somewhat surprisingly,
Eupetes is a sister taxon to Chaetops and Picathartes. The
discuss
the possible basis for this unusual and apparently relictual geographical
distribution. Jonsson KA, Fjeldså J, Ericson PGP, & Irestedt M.
"Systenatic placement of an enigmatic Southeast Asion taxon Eupetes macrocerus
and implications for the biogeography of a main songbird radiation, the Passerida."
Biology Letters Phylogeny (Royal Society). pp.1-4. (online
doi:10.1098rsbl.2007.0054).
    • 

  • Some nomenclatural difficulties arise as the H&M Checklist include a family
Eupetidae that contains Androphobus,
Psophodes, Ptilorrhoa and Eupetes. It seems this polyphyletic
assemblage is based on the convenience of geographical and superficial morphological
similarity, and that the actual phylogeny is quite different.

Calonectris edwardsii Systematics

    

  • Formerly considered conspecific with Calonectris diomedea.
    • 

  • Patterson JB, Armistead G. 2004. "First record of Cape Verde Shearwater
(Calonectris edwardsii) for North America." North American Birds
58:468-473. present discussion of the rationale for recognition of this taxon
at the species level. Morphologic and vocalization differences are felt to be
convincing for its distinction as a species.
    • 


  • Neither the 47th Supplement to the AOU Checklist, nor Patteson &
Armistead 2004 cite Bourne's 1986 discussion of this taxon and the possibility of
it's being a full species. Bourne, WRP. 1986. "Recent work on the origin and
suppression of bird species in the Cape Verde Isands, especially the shearwaters,
the herons, the kites and the sparrows." BBOC 106(4):163-170.. In that
work Bourne treats the form as a subspecies and suggests that there is clinal
variation in the morphology, and that the birds are "all very similar in behaviour
and ecology." 
    • 

  • In the 1986 paper, Bourne misspells the (sub)specific epithet "edwardsi"
with only one "-i".
    •

Buteo lineatus elegans Citation

    

  • Cited by Peters Checklist 1(2):364 as:

and Amadon in preparing this portion of the Peters Checklist did not take the
trouble to see what the AOU Checklist committee had resolved, especially as Amadon
himself was on that Committee.
