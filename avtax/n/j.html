Ammodramus maritimus juncicola Nomenclature
  • Originally spelled Passherbulus maritimus juncicola (note the two c's).
  • This spelling is correctly included in the AOU CL 5th:596 (1957).
  • Peters Checklist 13:75 (= Paynter, 1970) emends this without comment to Passherbulus maritimus junicola (note the single -c), and this is followed, without comment by H&M 3rd:75 (through Corrigenda 6).
  • The original description notes that the bird is 
    confined to dense patches of Juncus roemerianus ...
    and thus the name juncicola makes good sense, while junicola does not.
  • Great thanks to David Nicolson of ITIS for picking this up.

Loxia curvirostris japonica Nomenclature
  • Peters Checklist 14:290 (=Howell, et al., 1968) cites this to 1885 rather than to 1884, though no data in support of this date is provided.
  • The Richmond Index shows that this was published April 28, 1884 and additionally indicates that the name is included in a footnote -- a point not mentioned by Howell et al..
  • Morioka et al. 2005. "Types of Japanese Birds" p.61 correctly note the date as 1884. Those authors rely, at least in part, on Deignan's Type list for the Natl. Museum, who in turn relied heavily (and appropriately) on the Richmond Index.

Heliodoxa jacula jamersoni Nomenclature
  • Generally (and understandably) listed as jamesoni.
  • Peters Checklist 5:89 has
    Trochilus Jamersoni [sic. err. typ.] Bourcier ...
    and this is followed by most authors.
    • However the text of Bourcier's article makes no mention of "Jameson", so I believe the spelling must stand as originally printed.
  • The Richmond Index does not have a card for the Compte Rendu description, but of interest, has a card for 
    	Tr[ochilus] jamersoni	 M.J. Bourcier	 1851
	Rev. et mag. de Zoolog., Feb., 1851, p. 97
    So this "jamersoni" spelling (with the "r") evidently is not unique to the Compte Rendu, a fact which would appear to decrease the probability that it is simply a typographic error by the printer.
  • Normand David provides additional information (2007.01.18):
    From external sources of information, it is now apparent that Bourcier's jamersoni CRASP
32:187 can be linked to one William Jameson, Professor at the University of Quito.
Gould (1861), pl.95, in listing Heliodoxa jamesoni, or Jameson's Brilliant, writes "In the
naming of this bird after Professor Jameson of Quito, M. Bourcier has my entire acquiescence...
Happily for science, Professor Jameson has long been located at Quito..."
    [APP: "entire acquiescence" -- an interesting phrase.]
  • Thanks to Colin Jones for bringing this problem initially to my attention.

Melospiza melodia juddi Systematics
  • Held to be a distinct subspecies by Peters Checklist 13:47 (1970), and by H&M 3rd:781.
  • Held to be a synonym of M.m.melodia by Arcese, P., M. K. Sogge, A. B. Marr, and M. A. Patten. 2002. Song Sparrow (Melospiza melodia). In The Birds of North America, No. 704 (A. Poole and F. Gill, eds.). The Birds of North America, Inc., Philadelphia, PA. They cite Am. Ornithol. Union 1957, Todd 1963, Browning 1978, and Patten 2001. Of these references, AOU 1957, and Todd 1963 certainly treat this as a full subspecies. The other references have not (to date 2006.12.25) been examined.
  • Arcese et al. comment:
    Recognition of subspecies through much of the east "extend[s] the
trinomial system to the limits of utility" (Mengel 1965: 511). Shortly
after its description, Ridgway (1901: 358) synonymized M.m.juddi
with M.m.melodia, remarking that specimens from Atlantic Coast
and Great Plains "average slightly grayer than those from the
intermediate region, but the difference is so slight and inconstant that
subspecific separation seems to me unjustifiable." Bull (1974: 600)
considered neither M.m.euphonia nor M.m.juddi valid,
noting the "poorly differentiated" M.m.euphonia "is merely
part of an east-west cline extending from New England and New York . . .
through several slightly differentiated populations including the more
western . . . juddi (itself part of the cline)."
2006.12.25
Iridosornis jelskii Nomenclature
  • Peters Checklist 13:337 (Storer, 1970) lists the authority for Iridisornis jelskii in parentheses as "(Cabanis)", and lists the original combination correctly as Iridornis Jelskii.
    • My interpretation is that Iridornis is an emendation of Iridisornis Lesson, 1844. In the Falkenstein and Cabanis article, I do not find designation of the authority for the genus group name Iridornis but it is probably appropriate to attribute it to Bonaparte Ann.Sci.Nat. (Zool.) 4 ser. 1 p.127 where he attributes the name to "Less.".
  • The Richmond Index lists Iridornis Bonaparte as an emendation of Iridisornis Lesson, as does Neave's Nomenclator Zool.
  • The Code 1999 Art. 51.3.1 makes it clear that in the case of an incorrect spelling or emendation of a genus group name, the parentheses are not to be used.
  • H&M 3rd:809 follows the Peters Checklist in employing parentheses, and no comment is made on this in the Corrigenda (through Corr. 5).
  • Thanks to Colin Jones for picking this up.

2005.05.01
J.Orn. volume 7 Date
  • The numbers of the J.Orn. almost certainly never were publishe on the "specified date" during the first 50 years or so of publication. The delay was usually months, and not infrequently as many as 6 months or more. However, proof of delay is much less frequent than the fact of it.
  • The Volume 7, number 24 has a specified date of November, 1856, but contains material dated 16 Feb. 1857.

Ninox japonicus Citation
  • King (King B. 2002. "Species limits in the Brown Boobok Ninox scutulata Complex." BBOC 122(4):250-257) gives the citation as "N. s. japonica (Temminck and Schlegel) 1844" (p.251). This is the same as the HBW 5:233,688 citation, which gives FaunaJap. 1844 p.28.
  • Edward Dickinson indicates (in litt. 2003.01.26) 
    "The citation should be:

Strix hirsuta japonica Temminck & Schlegel, 1845, in Siebold, Fauna Japonica, Aves, p.28, pl.9B.

Note: Page 28 appeared in fascicle 2 in July 1845; 
	plate 9B appeared no earlier than 1849 (Holthuis & Sakai, 1970)."
    The footnote in H&M 3rd:234 reads:
    12This name must date from 1845 see Holthuis & Sakai (1970).
    Holthuis LB, Sakai T. 1970. "Ph. F. Von Siebold and Fauna Japonica. A History of Early Japanese Zoology." Academic Press of Japan. Tokyo, Japan.
  • So what evidence is to be found for 1844 vs 1845 for p.28 of this work?
    • Holthuis & Sakai 1970 (the authority Dickinson cites for the position that this "must be dated from 1845") give pagination for the fascicle (p.74) as
      	fasc. 1, pp. 1-24.	pls. 1,2,5,6,8,9,11,12,13, 17	1844 (14 July 1845)
	fasc. 2, pp. 25-44.	pls. 21,22,25,28,32,35,40,41,
				52, suppl. B	14 July 1845
				...
      [The date in parentheses apparently is a date given by the "Rijksarchief", apparently the date when the material was "sent out to Dutch libraries by the Dutch Government" and are presumed to be later than the "actual date of publication."]

      Holthuis also states (p.74)

      	The contents of the fascicles in many cases is incompletely 
known. As far as the text is concerned I have not been able to add
anything to Esaki's list. But in checking the parts of the Archiv für
Naturgeschichte containing information on the bird volume of the 
Fauna Japonica (see Sherborn & Jentink, 1895. Proc. zool. Soc. 
London. p.149), it proved possible to ascertain of many plates in 
which fascicle or groups of fascicles they were published.
    • Sherborn & Jentink, 1895 interprets the distribution of pages within fascicles differently from Holthuis, showing fascile I as pp.1-28 (not pp.1-24). This is footnoted by Sherborn and Jentink. The footnote indicates 
      Wiegmann [= the Archives für Naturgeschichte] says Pernis apivorus closed this part, 
but G.R. Gray, Genera, i. p.38, Sept. 1845, quotes p.25 and therefore 
includes signature G in part 1.
    • Holthuis makes no mention of this footnote, of Gray's interpretation, of Sherborn's interpretation, or of "signature G". These issues may be addressed in "Esaki's list" (not seen). However, as no reference is made to this footnote or to the issue, it appears to me that Holthuis did not read, or perhaps did not understand Sherborn & Jentink's footnote; for his results he would have had to ignore, or explicitly disagree with Sherborn & Jentink's interpretation, which clearly is pp."1-28" for fascicle 1 (1844). It is not surprising that Holthuis' examination of the Archives would lead him to his interpretation. Sherborn examined the same source, but aware of the data that Holthuis followed, they explicitly disagreed with what Holthuis' interpretation would be.
    • Sherborn dates the name on p.28 to 1844 in his Index Animalium, a fact not addressed by Holthuis or by Dickinson.
  • It appears to me that uncertainty remains regarding the date of this taxon and there is evidence supporting both 1844 and 1845. I do not feel compelled to follow a work that apparently is unaware of, does not understand, or ignores one of the fundamental conflicts at play here.
  • Perhaps Esaki's publiction (not seen) answers these questions. Edward Dickinson kindly provided the details of that citation (though I think he has not seen it). It is: Esaki, T., 1935. Zur Einführung in Philipp Franz von Siebolds 'Fauna Japonica'. 1-54, pl. 51-58. -- [publisher unkown], Tokyo..

Mirafra javanica Concept
  • Dickinson & Dekker. 2001. Discuss Mirafra javanica Horsfield, 1821. ( Dickinson EC & Dekker RWRJ. "Systematic notes on Asian birds. 11. A preliminary review of the Alaudidae" 2001. Zoologische Verhandelingen 335:61-63).
  • They favor a broad concept of M. javanicus including M. cantillans Blyth, 1845 (as the Peters [Mayr & Greenway] treatment does) until a more thorough presentation of the nature of the relationship is published.
  • Dickinson and Dekker 2001 further indicate that this broad concept of M. javanicus may well be eventually be split into three species.
    • Mirafra simplex (Heuglin) 1868 J.Orn. 16 p.226 [Afro-Arabian]
    • Mirafra cantillans Blyth 1845 J.Asiat.Soc.Bengal 13(1844) p.960 [Indian]
    • Mirafra javanica Horsfield 1821 Trans.Linn.Soc.London(1) 13 p.159 [Australasian]
