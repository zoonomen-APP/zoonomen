Actinodura egertoni khasiana Citation
  • In an interesting sidelight, The citation as give by Deignan in Peters Checklist 10:391 is a little peculiar. It is given as 
    	A[ctinura]. kansiana Godwin-Austen, 1876, Asiat. Soc. Bengal, 45, p.76
    of course this is usually given as the Journal of the Asiat. Soc. of Bengal (see for example Peters Checklist 10:246). This would be of minor interest except for the fact that Deignan almost (I stress almost) exactly reproduces the anomalous entry given in the Richmond Index, where the citation is given as 
    	A[ctinura] kansiana  H.H.Godwin-Austen, 1876, Asiat. Soc. Bengal 
	Journal, Vol.45 Pt.2 no.2, p.76
    Note that in the Richmond Index "Journal" is added at the end of the string, instead of in it's usual position.
  • It is probable that Deignan, working at the Smithsonian, took advantage of the Richmond Index, a possibility supported by this finding.
  • No mention of the Richmond Index is made in the Introduction to this Volume of Peters. This would hardly be the first (or the last) time that authors have paraded the findings from the Richmond Index as being the results of their own labors and understandings.
2009.03.05
Pycnonotus aurigaster klossi Nomenclature
  • The facts of the matter are as follows:
    1. The description of this taxon in BBOC 1920 Nov. 9th issue 41:12 is in the report of a meeting. The description was preceeded by text reading:
      	Mr. Stuart Baker on behalf of Mr. Robinson also described:--
    Molpastes atricapillus klossi subsp. nov.
      a description and additional information follows, and note is made that Nils Gyldenstolpe collected the type on the 10th May, 1914.
    2. The text is as follows:
    3. Then, a little over a year later, in the Nov. 29th, 1921 issue of the Bull.Brit.Orn.Cl. 42:32 there is an Erratum reading as follows:
      "Erratum. -- Count Nils Gyldenstolpe writes to point out
that, through an error on the part of Mr. Stuart Baker, his
description of Molpastes atricapillus klossi was attributed to
Mr. H. C. Robinson (cf. Bull. B. O. C. xli. p. 12, 1920)."
      .
  • I had confusions over authorial attribution here and was helped by Laurent Raty (email 2008.11.08) who pointed out that as this could be interpreted as occuring in the report of a meeting. This issue is addressed by Article 50.2. :
    50.2.Authorship of names in reports of meetings. If the name of a taxon is made available by publication in a report 
or minutes of a meeting, the person responsible for the name, not the Secretary or other reporter of the meeting, 
is the author of the name.
    Baker, was a participant in the meeting (not the Secretary, or other reporter) but it may be reasonable to see the issue as one of determining who is "responsible for the name", and it is further reasonable to hold this to be Gyldenstolpe. Though it must be noted that this case does not appear to be specifically the kind of problem intended to be addressed by 50.2

Rhipidura albiscapa keasti Citation
  • Peters Checklist 11:545 cites this as:
    Rhipidura fuliginosa keasti Ford, 1982, Emu, 81 (1981), p. 129
    clearly indicating the belief that it was not published until 1982.
  • H&M 3rd p.495 dates this to 1981, without comment.
  • The basis for the dating by the Peters Checklist authors is not clear, though Julian Ford himself was one of the reviewers of this volume of the checklist.
  • 1981 may be correct, but until further data comes forward to refute the dating by the Peters Checklist authors (as presumably reviewed by Ford) I will keep this as 1982.

Centropus sinensis kangeangensis Nomenclature
  • Often (Peters 4:71, H&M 3rd p.216, HBW 4:589) spelled kangeanensis (with one "g").
  • As Oscar van Rootselaar pointed out, the original spelling (which occurs multiple times in the text) is kangeangensis (with two g's). This same spelling is also employed on p.188 for another species name.
  • Some may wish to consider "prevailing usage" as supporting the incorrect spelling "kangaensis". I consider the "prevailing usage" of the ICZN 1999 to be a very poor, and poorly thought out idea. But that is not important. If "prevailing usage" is to be invoked here it can be so only on the basis of a published argument with adequate supporting data. To my knowledge, no such published position exists and thus the original spelling is appropriate to use.
  • Normand David helpfully points out that the Type list for birds in the Natural History Museum Leiden explicitly indicates that the spelling "kangeanensis" is an error (p.130 of the Type list).

2005.12.04
Hieraaetus kienerii Author
  • Authority for this name is given variably to de Sparre or to "G.S.=Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire".
  • Edward Dickinson has carefully pursued this matter, and has determined that the earlier convention of crediting de Sparre is supportable. The inclusion of Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire into the mix (though there is confusion about whether it is Isadore or Etienne) results from apparent misinterpretation of the initials "G. S."
  • Dickinson's details are in press, and we can be glad that he has cleared up this confusion.

Aglaiocercus kingii Nomenclature
  • Listed by Peters Checklist 5:124 as Aglaiocercus kingi (with one -i), and this is followed by HBW 5:659.
  • Peters Checklist 5:124 lists the original combination as Trochilus kingi Lesson, though in fact the orginal combination appears to be Ornismya kingii (with a double -i). This apparent error looks to have started with Cat.BirdsBrit.Mus. 16:137 where Salvin lists "Trochilus kingi" in the synonomy of Cyanolesbia gorgo.
  • Zimmer lists "Ornismya kingii Lesson" correctly when he proposes it as the type for Aglaiocercus on p.291 of his 1930 FieldMus.Nat.Hist.Pub.Zool.Ser. 17.

Caprimulgus indicus kelaarti Date
  • Uncertainties remain regarding the dates of the J.Asiat.Soc.Bengal. However, while Peters Checklist 4:204 dates this "1851 (1852)" and this 1852 date is followed by H&M 3rd:242, HBW 5:359.
  • In the above works several other taxa from the same Journal and later page numbers are attributed to 1851 (e.g. Garrulax cinereifrons).
  • The Richmond cardex lists the date as 1851, with a note "Feb. or later".
  • It is not certain when the volume was published, and it may end up being 1852 but until more facts become known, I will list this taxon as published in 1851. If 1852 is to be held as the date of publication, other taxa will need their dates of publication changed accordingly.

    1167. MALACOPTERON,  Eyton, 1839.

...

    • "MYIOTHERA leucostigma, Müll. Mus. Lugd. ex Born."
    which seems to be a nomen nudum

Dendrocopos kizuki 1836 Citation
  • Peters Checklist 6:201 has 1836, but no plate reference. Remarkably this is correct, though the majority of the other Peters entries for this livraison list "1835".
  • I follow {Richmond, et al. 1992} and {Sherborn, 1902} for the plate.
    • .
  • Dickinson EC (2001) points out that Mees (1994) provides evidence for 1836.
    • Dickinson EC. 2001. 'Systematic notes on Asian birds. 9. The "Nouveau recueil de planches coloriees" of Temminck & Laugier (1820-1839)' Zool. Verh., Leiden 335 p.7-56'
  • To quote Dickinson: 'Mees (1994) reported that an "Avis accompagnant la 97e livraison" was present in the copy of the "Planches coloriées" in Leiden and that this carries the date April 1836. It follows that the dates for livraisons 98 and 99 must also date from 1836, presumably from after April, and thus from December 31.'
    • Mees, G.F., 1994. "Vogelkundig onderzoek op Nieuw Guinea in 1828. Terugblik op de ornithologische resultaten van de reis van Zr. Ms. Korvet Triton naar de zuid-west kust van Nieuw-Guinea." Zool. Bijdr. Leiden 40: 1-64, fig. 1-8, colour pl. 1-12. (noot 15).
    • I interpret Avis to mean "a sort of preface".
  • Why Peters got this date right is an interesting mystery.
  • HBW 7:475 (Short & Horne) however revert to the erroneous "1835". This suggests they are not uncritically following the Peters Checklist (which is good), though they replicate other errors from that source.
