  • Pternistis afer (Muller,PLS) 1776 Natursyst.Suppl. p.129

    A confusing situation here:

  • Peters entry for the Type of Pternistis (2:84) reads "Type by subsequent designation, Tetrao capensis Gmelin = Tetrao afer P.L.S. Muller. (G.R.Gray, ListGen.BirdsEd.2 1841 p.79.)"
  • Peters lists Tetrao capensis Gmelin in Francolinus and Tetrao afer Muller in Pternistis(!).
  • If Peters considered T. capensis Gmelin, 1789 a junior synonym of T. afer Muller, 1776 it was not clear to me why he would list T. capensis as a species in Francolinus.
  • It seemed to me that Peters interpreted Gray to have designated T. capensis Gmelin, as the type for Pternistis. The text from Gray's 1841 work on p.79 is faithfully reproduced as follows: 
    PTERNISTIS, Wagl. (1832). Tetrao, Gm.  Perdix, Lath.  Fran-
	colinus, Steph.
  P. capensis, (Gm.) Wagl.  Forst.Dr.135.
  P. Cranchii, (Leach) Wagl.  P. punctulata, J.E.Gray. Ill.Ind.
	Zool.,pl.
    The basis for Peters interpretation of capensis as the type is based on its initial position in the sequence, which I understand from Murray Bruce and others is taken to be the convention at that time for type designation. I infered that T. capensis Gmelin was held (? by Peters) to be a synonym of T. afer Muller.

  • Ogilvie-Grant in Cat.BirdsBr.Mus. 22 (1893) p.173 gives the type for Pternistes as P. nudicollis (see below)
  • Peters comments on Pternistis nudicollis in a footnote 2:86 The footnote to Pternistis afer castaneiventer Gunning and Roberts, 1911 (2:86) says:
    "Replaces Pternistis nudicollis of authors, not Boddaert."
  • Currently I do not list any taxon with the specific epithet "castaneiventer". Peters holds castaneiventer to be a subspecies of afer, and I interpreted afer to be Peters' type for Pternistis. This is consistent with his "type" statement.
  • The synonomy for Pternistes nudicollis (held by Ogilvie-Grant to be the type) in Cat.BirdsBr.Mus. 22 p.174-5 is reproduced here:

  • Thus, it looked to me as if Peters made at least one, and possibly several errors:
    • If Tetrao capensis Gmelin is the type (and a synonym for Tetrao afer Muller), it can't be listed as a species in Francolinus
    • Peters does not seem to have determined the status of the name Tetrao nudicollis Boddaert, which it seems may have standing as the type for Pternistis.
  • Why Peters does not follow Ogilvie-Grant's interpretation of the type for Pternistes is not clear.

  • The Richmond Index lists Pternistis Wagler and indicates 3 included species.
    1. "Perdix capensis auct.
    2. Perdix cranchi Tuckey.
    3. Pt. rubricollis (Rüpp. Zool. Atl. t.30)."
  • Peters lists Perdix cranchii Leach, in Tuckey, 1818 as a subspecies: Pternistis afer cranchii (2:85).

  • The Richmond index lists two emendations of Pternistis
    • Pternistes by Gray, 1840 ListGen.Birds p.61
    • Pternestes by Blyth, 1855 J.As.Soc.Bengal 24 p.304
  • Jobling (1991, p.192) gives the derivation of the name as:
    • "Pternistis Gr. pternistes one who trips with the heel; ref. the double spurs of the cock spurfowl."
  • Rendering of the Greek in such a manner may have accounted for Gray's emendation.

... the name of the type has been applied to more than one taxon over time. The two species that must NOT be confused are:

  • Tetrao capensis Gmelin, 1789, Syst. Nat., 1, p. 759. Cape Francolin.
    Francolinus capensis (Gmelin, 1789).
  • Tetrao afer P. L. S. Müller, 1776, Syst. Nat., suppl., p. 129. Red-necked Spurfowl Pternistis afer (P. L. S. Müller, 1776)

    • synonyms:
    • Tetrao nudicollis Boddaert, 1783, Pl. Enl., p. 11, pl.180.
    • Tetrao rubricollis Gmelin, 1789, Syst. Nat., 1, p.758.

What confuses things is that when Wagler established Pternistis, he called this bare-throated taxon 'Pternistis capensis' based on part of Stephens in Shaw's (1819) 'Francolinus capensis', so if this was quoted in full it would be something like:

  • Pternistis capensis Wagler, 1832 (non Tetrao capensis Gmelin, 1789)
This is why Richmond listed the first included species as 'Perdix capensis auct.' i.e. 'of authors'.
Peters' note (1934: 86) that Pternistis afer castaneiventer 'replaces' Pternistis nudicollis of authors, not Boddaert, is designed to catch out the unwary, as the principle of coordination treats all species-group taxa as 'equals' i.e. P. nudicollis can only be the junior synonym of P. afer afer: P. afer castaneiventer is a different taxon. The same is true of P. afer cranchii.

If Peters had copied the citation in W. L. Sclater (1930: 90) properly he would have put
P. capensis Wagl. = Tetrao afer Müll.
in the check-list, and not what he did put -- saving you a great deal of trouble!

Assuming that the above is correct the citation should be:

  • Pternistis Wagler, 1832, Isis von Oken, 25, col. 1229.
    • Type by subsequent designation G.R.Gray, 1841, A List of the Genera of Birds, ed. 2, p. 79.
    • P.[ternistis] capensis (Gm.[elin]) Wagl.[er] = Tetrao afer P.L.S.Müller
      Pternistis afer (P.L.S.Müller)

Wagler's name 'P. capensis' for the first included species is not Tetrao capensis Gmelin, 1789. G. R. Gray lists this name first, with the other names belonging to a taxon only subspecifically distinct.

A certain 'leeway' is given to pre-1931 names, both in what constitutes a valid description, and a subsequent designation.
