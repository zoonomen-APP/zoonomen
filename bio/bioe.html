Emin

	Emin Bey
	Emin Pasha
	Christened Eduoard (Eduard) Carl Oscar Theodor Schnitzer.

	b. 28 March, 1840 Oppelen Silesia (Germany)
	d. 23 or 24 Oct., 1892 (murdered) at Kinena ~ 80 mi ESE of Stanley Falls, Congo.
	The hame "Emin" he adopted, it was the name of a Doctor that he replaced 
	in Lado.
	He is frequently referred to as "Emin Pasha", but "Pasha" is atitle of rank in the Ottoman
	empire. General Gordon, for example was often referred to as "Gordon Pasha" but no one
	would include "Pasha" as part of his name.  

	Hartlaub refers to this man as "Emin Bey" similar to "Pasha" "Bey" is a Turkish title
	for "Chieftan" and it seems to me should not be held as part of a person's name.

	[Thanks to Gastone Rabascini for additional details]
